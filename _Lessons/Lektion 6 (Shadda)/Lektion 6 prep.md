# Shadda
In most languages, a letter could be emphasized or made stronger or longer. This usually happens when a letter is doubled, so there are two of it in sequence.
In pronunciation, this is very very important, if you get it wrong, the word either sounds wrong, the person may not understand what you are saying, or the meaning of the word may completely change.
Let's show a few examples in German:
## Emphasis in German
- Mitte / Miete
- bitten / bieten
- bitten / biten (wrong form, but useful to show contrast)
- Kasse / Käse
- Ratte / Rate
- lassen / lasen
- hassen / Hasen
- still / Stil
- Lippe / Liebe
- Schuss / Schuh
## English
- unnamed / unaimed
- misspell / mispell
- bookkeeper / bookeeper
- midday / mid-day
- unknown / unown
- illegal / ileal
- unnecessary / unecessary
- immoral / imoral
- roommate / روم-mate (spoken emphasis on “m”)

_(Note: English has fewer true minimal pairs; emphasis often appears across word boundaries or prefixes.)_

# Arabic
In Arabic, _Shadda_ means a letter is **stronger / doubled**. If you don’t emphasize it, the word can sound wrong or even change meaning.
## Lets look at a few examples from words we've already learned

- Tfaddal (m) / Tfaddali (w) (تفضل / تفضلي)  
- Rijjal رجال
- Hubb حب
- Sabiyet صبية
