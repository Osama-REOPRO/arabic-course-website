# Vocabulary: Family Members (extended)

### Core Family
* **2ab**: Father 
* **imm**: Mother 
* **AhI**: Parents ($imm + 2ab = AhI$) 
* **2a5**: Brother 
* **i5t**: Sister 
* **i5wat**: Siblings ($2a5 + i5t = i5wat$) 

### Grandparents
* **Jidd**: Grandfather 
* **Sitt**: Grandmother 

### Paternal Side (Father's side)
* **3amm**: Paternal Uncle (Father's brother) 
* **3ammet**: Paternal Aunt (Father's sister) 
* **ibn 3amm**: Paternal Cousin (Male) 
* **bint 3amm**: Paternal Cousin (Female) 

### Maternal Side (Mother's side)
* **5al**: Maternal Uncle (Mother's brother) 
* **5alet**: Maternal Aunt (Mother's sister) 
* **ibn 5al**: Maternal Cousin (Male) 
* **bint 5al**: Maternal Cousin (Female) 

### Friends
* **Rfi2**: Friend (m) 
* **Rfi2at / Rif2at**: Friends (m/f)

# Grammar: Attached Pronouns (Damir muttasil) 

When attached to a noun (**ism**), these suffixes indicate possession:

| Independent Pronoun | Suffix    | Example                |
| :------------------ | :-------- | :--------------------- |
| **ana** (I)         | **+ i**   | **ismi** (My name)     |
| **inte** (You, m)   | **+ ak**  | **ismak** (Your name)  |
| **inti** (You, f)   | **+ ik**  | **ismik** (Your name)  |
| **huwe** (He)       | **+ o**   | **ismo** (His name)    |
| **hiye** (She)      | **+ a**   | **isma** (Her name)    |
| **ni7na** (We)      | **+ na**  | **ismna** (Our name)   |
| **intu** (You all)  | **+ kon** | **ismkon** (Your name) |
| **hinnen** (They)   | **+ on**  | **ismon** (Their name) |

# Formulas for Making sentences using pronouns/suffixes
## Who is this? / Who are those? (question)
> [!NOTE] Formula
> **Mien + had/hay/hadol?**

you point at a person or more and ask "who is this / who are those?"
##### Examples
* mien hadol? (Who are these?) 
* mien hay? (Who is this (f)?)
* mien had? (Who is this (m)?)

## This is [noun] / Those are [noun] (answer)
Note: this is the answer to the previous question, but this is also how you ask a (yes/no) question, the exact same formula but with a question mark at the end! (you will see that a bit later)
> [!NOTE] Answer Formula: 
> **Had/Hay/Hadol + noun**

you point at a person or more and say "that is [noun] / those are [noun]"
or if you are asking a yes/no question you do the exact same thing, you point at a person or more and say "is that [noun]? / are those [noun]?" (in arabic the only change is your tone)
##### Examples
* had karim (this is karim)
* had 25i M7ammad (that is my brother Muhammad)
* hadol immi w 2abi. (These are my mother and my father.) 
* hadol 3aylti. (These are my family.) 
	* Note: this can also be done in singular "Hay 3aylti (this is my family)", both are the same
* Hadol Ahl 2abi. (These are my father's parents.) 
* Hay immak (that is your mother)
* Hinnin Ahli. (They are my parents.) 
---
## Is this [noun/pronoun]? / Are those [noun/pronoun]? (yes/no question)
notice how this is exactly the same as the previous formula just with a question mark at the end!
And in spoken form you simply *say it* like a question, you change your tone, and that is the equivalent of a question mark!
> [!NOTE] Yes/No Question Formula: 
> **Had/Hay/Hadol + noun + ?**
##### Examples
- had karim? (is that karim?)
- had 25i M7ammad? (is that my brother Muhammad?)
- hadol immi w 2abi? (Are these my mother and my father.) 
- hadol 3aylti? (Are these my family.) 
- Hadol Ahl 2abi? (Are these my father's parents.) 
- Hay immak? (Is that your mother)
## Yes/No, this is / those are [noun] (yes/no answer)
> [!NOTE] Yes/No Answer Formula: 
> **(positive): Eh, Had/Hay/Hadol + noun**
> **(negative): La2, Had/Hay/Hadol + *moo* + noun**
##### Examples
- had karim? (is that karim?)
    - Eh, had karim. (yes, that is karim.)
    - La2, had moo karim. (no, that is not karim.)
- had 25i M7ammad? (is that my brother Muhammad?)
    - Eh, had 25i M7ammad. (Yes, that is my brother Muhammad.)
    - La2, had moo 25i M7ammad. (no, that is not my brother Muhammad.)
- hadol immi w 2abi? (Are these my mother and my father.)
    - Eh, hadol immi w 2abi. (Yes, these are my mother and my father.)
    - La2, hadol moo immi w 2abi. (no, these are not my mother and my father.)
- hadol 3aylti? (Are these my family.)
    - Eh, hadol 3aylti. (Yes, these are my family.)
    - La2, hadol moo 3aylti. (no, these are not my family.)
- Hadol Ahl 2abi? (Are these my father's parents.)
    - Eh, hadol Ahl 2abi. (Yes, these are my father's parents.)
    - La2, hadol moo Ahl 2abi. (no, these are not my father's parents.)
- Hay immak? (Is that your mother)
    - Eh, hay immak. (Yes, that is your mother)
    - La2, hay moo immak. (no, that is not your mother)
- Had inte? (Is this you?)
	* Eh, hada ana. (Yes, this is me.) 
	* La2, hada moo ana. (no, this is not me.) 
- Hadol Jiddak w Sittak? (are those your granpa and grandma?)
	* Eh, hadol Jiddi w Sitti. (Yes, these are my grandfather and grandmother.) 
	* La2 hadol moo Jiddi w Sitti. (No, these are not my grandfather and grandmother.)
	* Notice the suffixes are added to both words

## What is [pronoun]'s name? (question)

> [!NOTE] Question Formula: 
> **Shu + ism[suffix]?**
##### Examples
- Shu ismak? (what is your name? (m))
- Shu ismek? (what is your name? (f))
- Shu ismo? (what is his name?)
- Shu isma? (what is her name?)
- Shu ismkon? (what is your name? (p))
- Shu ismna? (what is our name?)
- Shu ismon? (what is their name?)
## [pronoun]'s name is [noun] (answer)

> [!NOTE] Answer Formula: 
> **[pronoun] + ism[suffix] + [noun]**
##### Examples
- Shu ismak? (what is your name? (m))
	- Ana ismi Samy (my name is Sami)
- Shu ismek? (what is your name? (f))
	- Ana ismi Salma (my name is Salma)
- Shu ismo? (what is his name?)
	- Huwe ismo Samy (his name is Samy)
- Shu isma? (what is her name?)
	- Hiyye isma Salma (her name is Salma)
- Shu ismkon? (what is your name? (p))
	- ni7na ismna al-karame (our name is al-karameh (a football team in Syria))
- Shu ismna? (what is our name?)
	- ni7na ismna al-karame (our name is al-karameh (a football team in Syria))
	- same answer as before
- Shu ismon? (what is their name?)
	- hinnen ismon al-karame (their name is al-karameh (a football team in Syria))
## What is [noun]'s name? (question)

> [!NOTE] Question Formula: 
> **Shu + ism[suffix] + noun[suffix]**
>
> suffix attached to "ism" is chosen according to the noun
> suffix attached to noun is chosen according to the person we are speaking with

> [!WARNING] Warning: Difficult
> this is a bit more difficult than the other formulas, so first master the formulas above and once you are comfortable come back here

> [!WARNING] Warning: Strange
> this way of talking can be strange to non native speakers, this is how it would sound if you were doing the same thing in english:
> "what is (his name) (your father)?"
> "what is (her name) (your mother)?"
> "what is (their name) (his parents)?"
> but with practice you will get used to it


##### Examples
- Shu ismo 5alak? (what is your maternal uncle's name?)
- Shu isma immak? (what is your mothers name?)
- Shu ismon 2i5watak? (what is your siblings names?)
- Shu isma 3ammtak? (what is your paternal aunt's name?)

## [noun]'s name is [name]
> [!NOTE] Answer Formula: 
> **noun[suffix] + ism[suffix] + [name]**
>
> suffix attached to "ism" is chosen according to the noun
> suffix attached to noun is chosen according to the person who is answering

##### Examples
- Shu ismo 5alak? (what is your maternal uncle's name?)
	* 5ali ismo M7ammad. (My maternal uncle's name is Mohammad.) 
- Shu isma immak? (what is your mothers name?)
	- immi isma Sabiha (my mother's name is Sabiha)
- Shu ismon 2i5watak? (what is your siblings names?)
	- 2i5wati ismon Noor w Noora (my sibling's names are Noor and Noora)
- Shu isma 3ammtak? (what is your paternal aunt's name?)
	- 3ammti isma Sumayyah (My paternal aunt's name is Sumayyah)

## [name] is [pronoun]'s [family relation]? (yes/no question)
> [!NOTE] Question Formula: 
> **[name] + family relation[suffix]**
##### Example
- Kamal 3ammak? (is Kamal your uncle?)
- Manal 5altkon? (is Manal your maternal aunt? (p))
- Hadeel immo? (is Hadeel his mother?)
## [name] is [pronoun]'s [family relation]? (yes/no)
> [!NOTE] Question Formula: 
> **eh + [name] + family relation[suffix]**
> **la2 + [name] + moo + family relation[suffix]**
##### Example
- Kamal 3ammak? (is Kamal your uncle?)
	- eh, Kamal 3ammi (yes, kamal is my uncle)
	- la2, Kamal moo 3ammi (no, kamal is not my uncle)
- Manal 5altkon? (is Manal your maternal aunt? (p))
	- eh, Manal 5altna (yes, Manal is our aunt)
	- la2, Manal moo 5altna (no, Manal is not our aunt)
- Hadeel immo? (is Hadeel his mother?)
	- eh, Hadeel immo (yes hadeel is his mother)
	- la2, Hadeel moo immo (no hadeel is not his mother)
# Notes
### The use of "ya" (specifying who you are talking to)
this can be added at the end of any question, and is used to specify who we are talking to, if you want to mention the person you are talking with by name then you put "ya" before it, it is sort of like how you say: "I pray to you, O god" it is like that "O" (find an example in german)
At the end of any question you ask, you can always add "ya+name" to the end to kind specify who specifically you are speaking with 
##### Examples
- Had inte *ya Karim*? (Is this you, Karim?) 
- Hadol i5watek ya Zahra? (are those your siblings, Zahra?)
### Suffixes with Compound Nouns
Compound nouns are when you place one noun in front of another to specify it, for example:
- ibn 3amm (cousine / son of uncle / father side)
- Shantayet Madraset (school bag)

**Suffixes are placed at the second word**, that is, coumpounds are treated as a single unit.

For example:
- ibn 3amm**i** (my cousine)
- Shantayet Madraset**ak** (your school bag)

# General Examples 

### Q&A Matching 
* **Q**: mien hay? (Who is she?) -> **A**: Hay Rfi2ti Maya (This is my friend Maya) 
* **Q**: Hay immak ya karim? -> **A**: La2, hay 5alti. (No, she is my maternal aunt.)
