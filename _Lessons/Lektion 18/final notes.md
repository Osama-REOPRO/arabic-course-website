- the answer is that the job of the teacher is not limited to explaining things, that is in my opinion a very small portion of a teacher's job, especially today with the internet, no matter how good I am at explaining things, you will always be able to find a better explanation online.
- I think the much more important role for the teacher are the following:
# The job of the teacher
## To give direction
if you just go on your own, of course, theoretically, all the information is available, however, you wouldn't know where to start!
My job is to determine in which order we learn things, so you aren't overwhelmed with complexity, just look at all the horror stories online! (arabic can get extremely complex)
## Emotional Support
Don't laugh, I think a big big part of my job is just to acknowledge the difficulty of learning a language, especially one like arabic, just to acknowledge that it can really be difficult. I think when that is done you stop blaming yourself, you stop thinking: "there is something wrong with me". Which I think is the actual reason why most people quit and never actually learn the language. it isn't the actual difficulty of the language that drives them to quit, it is the emotional stress that a student goes through as they experience difficulty, the self judgement, the insecurity, the unfair comparisons with others.
that is to say: I am protecting you from overwhelming your self.
that is why I go much slower than other teachers, as I explained in the very beginning of the course.
