you don't have to memorize them at all! just check if the letter is pronounced with your tongue in the front of your mouth, from the same place that you pronounce the letter "L", if the letter is pronounced from the same place, then it is a sun letter, otherwise, it is a moon letter!
it is just difficult to pronounce two letters back to back from the same part of the mouth, our mouths are more comfortable alternating the places from which they make the sound, that way they can string voices back to back much more efficiently (show this on [[Anatomische Erklärung]], demonstrate this by pronouncing basically any word from any language), it is like it is faster to do repetetive things with two alternating hands rather than with a single hand
refer back to [[Anatomische Erklärung]] and look at how all the sun letters cluster around the front of the mouth, they are all letters pronounced very close to how the letter "L" is pronounced
if to know if a letter is a sun letter, just try pronouncing it, if it comes out from an area towards the front of the mouth with the tongue similar to "L" then it is a sun letter! easy as that!
and as time goes on you will instinctively find it awkward to pronounce them with a full "al-" if they are sun letters, which makes it natural
I myself can't remember which letters are sun letters and which are moon letters!
