# Pronouns & Possessive suffixes – Reference

Possession in Arabic is shown by a **suffix** on the noun (not a separate word like *my*).

> ism (*name*) + **i** = **ismi** (*my name*)

| Pronoun | | Suffix | Example (ism = name) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **ana** | I | **+i** | ismi (my name) |
| **inte** | you (m) | **+ak** | ismak (your name) |
| **inti** | you (f) | **+ik** | ismik (your name) |
| **huwe** | he | **+o** | ismo (his name) |
| **hiye** | she | **+a** | isma (her name) |
| **ni7na** | we | **+na** | ismna (our name) |
| **intu** | you (all) | **+kon** | ismkon (your name) |
| **hinnen** | they | **+on** | ismon (their name) |

> [!NOTE] Remember
> The same suffixes attach to **plural** words too:
> sayyaaraat + na = **sayyaaratna** (*our cars*), i5wat + i = **i5wati** (*my siblings*).
