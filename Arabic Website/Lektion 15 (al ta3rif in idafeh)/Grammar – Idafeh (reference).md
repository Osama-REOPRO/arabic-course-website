# Idafeh (Genitive construction) – Reference

The **Idafeh** joins two nouns into one unit (like the English *school bag* / German compound).

> **head noun + specifier** → Tabba5 + maT3am = **Tabba5 maT3am** (*a restaurant cook*)

## Rules

| Rule | |
| --- | --- |
| The **first** word **never** takes el- | |
| Definiteness is controlled by the **second** word | |
| second word **+ el-** → whole Idafeh **definite** | Tabba5 **el-**maT3am = *the cook of the restaurant* |
| second word **without el-** → **indefinite** | Tabba5 maT3am = *a restaurant cook* |
| **No pause** when speaking – it flows like one word | mudif-el-maT3am |

## Feminine words (ending -et/-at)

In an Idafeh the "t" becomes **audible**. Before el-, the "e" before the "t" also drops.

| Form | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| word alone | taalebet | a (female) student |
| Idafeh **without** el- | taalebet jaam3at | a university student |
| Idafeh **with** el- | **taalebt** ej-jaam3at | the student of the university |

> **Remember:** before el- the "e" has no room left → it drops: taleb**et** → tale**bt** el-…

## Examples

- baab beet → *a front door* · baab **el-**beet → *the front door*
- meftaa7 sayyarat → *a car key* · meftaa7 **es-**sayyarat → *the car key*
- ghurfet noom → *a bedroom* · ghurft **en-**noom → *the bedroom*

*(The second word follows the normal [[Grammar – al- (reference)|sun/moon letter rules]].)*
