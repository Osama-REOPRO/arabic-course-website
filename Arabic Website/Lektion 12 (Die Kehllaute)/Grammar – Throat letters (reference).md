# Throat letters & chat numbers – Reference

In **Arabizi** (Arabic written with Latin letters), the throat sounds are written with **numbers** that visually resemble the Arabic letter.

| Number | Letter | Sound | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **9** (q) | ق Qaaf | deep, hard "k" far back. **In Syria often pronounced like 2 (Hamza)** | Rfi9 / Rfi2 (friend) |
| **5** (kh) | خ Khaa | scraping "ch" as in Scottish *loch* / German *Bach* | 5al (uncle), Tabba5 (cook) |
| **4 / 8** (gh) | غ Ghayn | gurgling "r", like the French/German throat R | 4ali = غالي (expensive) |
| **7** | ح Haa | deep, breathy "h" (fogging up glasses), throat tight in the middle | 7alla2 (barber), Mar7aba |
| **3** | ع 3ayn | deep sustained squeeze of the mid-throat | 3ilbet (can), bayya3 (seller) |
| **2** | ء Hamza | glottal stop – a sudden stop of air | 2ab (father), 2anset (teacher) |
| **h** | ه Haa | ordinary relaxed "h" as in *house* | muhandes (engineer) |

> [!NOTE] Memory aid
> The numbers mimic the letters: **ح ≈ 7**, **خ ≈ 5** (Arabic ٥), **ق ≈ 9**, **ء ≈ 2**, **ع ≈ 3**.

**Four "cheat" letters** you already have in English/German: خ (loch/Bach sound), غ (throat R), ه (normal h) and ء (glottal stop, e.g. the catch in "uh‑oh").
