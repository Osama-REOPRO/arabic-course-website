# Exercises
## Saying "aaa" while opening the mouth wide and looking downwards
- Say "aaa" continuously while opening the mouth wide and slowly looking down, then as you go back up, the choked sound as it relieves will end up making the wanted "choking" sound "3ayn"
## The "Grossed Out" Sound (Das Ekel-Geräusch)
imagine you just bit into a rotten apple or saw something repulsive.
- make the sound: **"Iiiih!"** or **"Bäh!"**
- Now force that sound from deep in the throat, tightening the muscles as if you are about to gag.
- That sudden contraction in the mid-throat is the exact starting position for **ع**.
## The Doctor's Visit (Beim Arzt)
When a doctor looks down your throat, they say, _"Machen Sie 'Ah'."_
- Instruct your students to say a long **"Ah"**.
- While keeping the sound going, tell them to pretend someone is gently pressing down on the very back of their tongue with a spoon, or that they are trying to swallow the back of their tongue.
- The resulting "strangled" or compressed vowel is the sound of **ع**.
## The "Gargle" Transition (Vom R zum ع)
Most German students will automatically try to substitute **ع** with the German vocalic **R** (like in _Mutter_).
- make a continuous German **R** sound (as in the word _Krone_ or _Sprechen_).
- slowly slide that friction point **further down the throat**, away from the uvula (the little dangling thing) and into the main pipe.
## The "Fake Swallowing" Trick
swallow and pause mid-swallow.
- The throat closes tightly at that peak moment.
- try and speak or let air out right as they begin to release that tightness.
- the same muscles involved in swallowing are also involved in pronouncing the "3ayn"
# Vocab with "3ayn"
- 3arabi = arabic
- 3ammo = respectful "uncle"
- 3mr = age
- 3Tr = perfume
- 3seer = juice
- 3ilbe = bottle
- 3a2l = brain
- ma3 = with
- ba3d = after
- 3ayn = eye
- 3ala = on (compare with german "alle")
