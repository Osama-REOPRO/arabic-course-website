feminine words in general change in more complex ways than masculine words
- in an idafeh, the "t" at the end of "et/at" becomes pronounced
- specific to syria: the word on it's own will have "-at/et" at the end, but within an idafeh with voiced "t" it changes to "-et"
- if there is "al-" added after it within an idafeh, then the vowel is removed and it becomes just a "-t"
	- in reality if you listen carefuly, you will see that the change to the word is actually even more complex! but we will stop here to reduce overwhelm
	- 2anset > 2anset madraset > 2anest el-madraset

# steps
## without 'al-'
1. 't' becomes pronounced
2. convert 'at' to 'et'
## with 'al-'
1. 't' becomes pronounced
2. remove 'e'
