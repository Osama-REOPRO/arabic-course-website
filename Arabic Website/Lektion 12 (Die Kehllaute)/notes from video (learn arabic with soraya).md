# Q = ق
gutteral (k)
#### the difference from regular k
- regular k is pronounced using the back of the tongue and the back of the mouth roof
- gutteral k is not using the tongue at all, instead it is as if you are pronouncing k but from your throat, without your tongue involvement at all
	- the tongue stays out of the way completely
- gutteral k is deeper withing the throat, it is farther down, k is higher
#### Exercise
press with your finger down on your tongue and try to pronounce the regular (k), that will prevent the involvement of your tongue (although it will try to fight you!)
#### Examples
- Qalam = pen
- Waraq = paper
- Qalaq = anxiety (two times q)
- Qalb = heart
#### Regional differences
this letter is special and pretty significant in syria specifically, how you pronounce it tells other people where you are from
- if you are from the coast, sweida or the jazeera region, then you pronounce it hard and clear
- if you are from wester regions closer to lebanon, like damascus, homs, hamah, aleppo, then you actually mostly don't pronounce it and replace it with the easier "hamza"
- in lebanon, the letter is completely dropped
- in syria, even in regions where the q tends to get dropped, the letter is not gone entirely, it still shows up in some words, especially those coming from standard arabic, like (iqtisad = economy), so we are still going to learn it, so we can speak in any regional variety that we like

# H = ح = 7
it is like pronouncing the regular h while choking!
## Examples
- Hub = Love
- Harf = Letter
- Rooh = Soul
- Lahme = Meat

# Š = ص = 9
gutteral (s)
the difference from regular S is that your throat is involved here
#### Examples
- Šẽf = صيف = Summer
	- if you pronounce this with regular (s) the meaning changes and becomes "sword"
- Šoorat = صورة = Picture
- 2Šfar = أصفر = Yellow
- Furšat = فرصة = chance

# Ť = ط = 6

again, it is more gutteral
